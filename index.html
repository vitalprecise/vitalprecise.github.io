VisuoTactile Local (ViTaL) policies, an effective framework for precise, contact-rich manipulation that combines visuotactile learning, visual augmentation, and residual RL for highly robust, generalizable policies.

Abstract

Data-driven approaches struggle with precise manipulation: imitation learning requires a large number of hard-to-obtain demonstrations, while reinforcement learning yields brittle policies with limited generalization. We propose VisuoTactile Local (ViTaL) policy learning, a framework that leverages egocentric vision and tactile sensing to learn robust visuotactile policies that are consistent across task-irrelevant variations in the environment. By combining domain randomization with vision foundation models for segmentation, we demonstrate that a simple behavior cloning objective is sufficient to train robust, distractor-agnostic visual encoders. These encoders facilitate learning precise offset policies through reinforcement learning in fixed environments. Our experiments show that ViTaL policies achieve 90% success rates on fine manipulation tasks in unseen environments while being extremely robust to distractors. Through a careful ablation study, we validate each component of ViTaL and present a generic pipeline for solving precise, contact-rich tasks. Finally, we show that ViTaL policies can serve as low-level primitives within high-level VLMs, enabling skill reuse with significantly improved robustness and success rates compared to existing methods.
